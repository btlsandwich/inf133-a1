Random Facts About My Writing

(for fun)

Number Fact
1. "Spring Upon the Solstice" was originally inspired by a mix of Pacific Rim and Neon Genesis Evangelion. How? You'd have to read more to find out.
2. "Spring Upon the Solstice" has undergone five major draft revisions in its five years of conception. I am a perfectionist.
3. The majority of "Pride, a Peasant, and a Purpose" was written with an iPhone 5 on the 29 bus line on my way to school.
4. "cyberpunk_2020" was a placeholder name for "Neon Rain Over Erebus-IV" that I only kept because I wanted to give Cyberpunk 2077 a chance. Unfortunately, the two have no real connection.
5. An excerpt of "Neon Rain Over Erebus-IV" was published in the 2020 edition of my high school's literary magazine The Junkyard, and as such, I have neglected to "copy-paste" reproduce that excerpt on this site out of respect for the magazine.
6. "Three Nights in Kyoto" is partially inspired by real events. You can likely guess which part in the synopsis actually happened.
7. "Prayers for Mortality" takes cues from the video game lore of Genshin Impact, though this is entirely unintentional and I didn't even know it existed when I wrote up the concept.
8. Of the four novels that I have completed, only two are mentioned on this site. That is because I am social distancing from the other two for the sake of my pride.
