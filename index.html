Welcome to the blackrose archive.



My name is Brandon, and I am a third-year Game Development & Interactive Media major at UC Irvine.

This webpage is a bare-bones HTML spiritual clone of my real portfolio, which serves to showcase excerpts of the various literary works I have written over the last eight years, and summarize several more.

As implied before, I have been writing for six years. I started in middle school when a classmate approached me to co-write a novel, and although that turned out in disaster, the feeling of writing stuck with me.

It gave me a sense of fulfillment and direction. like a long-term goal to work towards. I ran with that, and since I began this journey, I've completed four full-length novels.

Admittedly, the number of scrapped projects and rejected ideas far outnumbers the amount that I have completed, but I think that's about par for the course. Let's take a look at some that did make the cut.

"Neon Rain Over Erebus-IV"
(2020-2022)


A disgraced cyborg bounty hunter named Kleo is dumped on a junkyard world and left for dead. However, she is saved by a wandering scavenger, who mistakes her for a god.
Together, the two plan to restore Kleo's honor and expose the guild that betrayed her.

Genre: action, science fiction, cyberpunk

"Spring Upon the Solstice"
(2016-2022)


A self-proclaimed philosopher seeks to uncover the truth behind his sister's death, only to find himself drafted into a supernatural war to prevent all other wars.
His only saving grace is a mysterious girl from the Japanese yakuza, hellbent on rescuing him from the conflict of man versus monster.

Genre: action, science fiction

"Pride, a Peasant, and a Purpose"
(2016-2019)


The noble son of the Arondight family discovers his familial connection to a band of criminals called the Arbiters, who were accused of assassinating the previous king.
After losing his home city and family to a surprise attack by an unknown enemy, he seeks out the Arbiters—the only potential allies he has left—in order to claim his vengeance.

Genre: medieval, dark fantasy

"Three Nights in Kyoto"
(2018-2020)


As World War III hits the Japanese homefront, more and more young individuals are summoned and whisked away to fight in a battle they have no stake in. In the meantime, the vice president of the student council fights in an unseen crusade to impress his crush.
Ultimately, he may be the catalyst to preventing Japan's nuclear retaliation and further loss of life, both in his home city of Kyoto and all across the world.

Genre: crime fiction, mystery, young adult

"Prayers for Mortality"
(2020-2022)


In this world, certain individuals are blessed by the goddesses to enact their divine will, but most have used their newfound power as mercenaries for hire, which resulted in a bloody power struggle that lasted years.
Within the peacetime, a veteran from the last war suddenly finds herself staring down the barrel of a full-scale rebellion against the divine.

Genre: fantasy, heresy, cosmic terror
