Chapter 1-1: "The Hero Deferred"

(This is only an excerpt.)

“So, how much longer until your guy gets here?”

“I dunno. The drug dealer said it’s usually around five o’clock. So ten minutes maybe?”

“I’m very glad we can trust the drug dealer.”

“Yeah, me too.”

“Well, nice. Ten more minutes before I regret all of my life decisions.”

“I was under the impression you didn’t mind a little risk.”

“Oh, I love risk alright, just not like this. Not on the word of some drug dealer I’ve never met.”

“We’ll be fine,” I assure him, as I reboot the PC before me and log in under a new list of network settings. A grin breaks through my glazed-over reverie; for the fourth time this week, I’ve been able to establish a stable connection to the Internet. My luck recently has amazed me.

“Sweet,” my friend says. “They still haven’t blocked this one yet.”

“Not like they would know where to look for these anyways. I scrolled through like ten pages of search results to find this host. No chance.”

I load up a browser and input some query terms, although I’m confident my luck will run out here. Ten whole sessions so far and nothing, no matter how wide of a net I cast. You would think that the Internet never forgets, and that somewhere among its petabytes of graveyards, of histories and empires and conversations long past, there is at least one trace of evidence to prove a specific someone once existed on this Earth. A social media handle, an obituary, or a picture would all suffice. But as I continue to dig deeper, I realize that perhaps my person of interest didn’t want to be found. If that is indeed the case, she did one hell of a job hiding her tracks.

My friend groans and rolls back his head. “Come on, Jhiro. News articles again? Really?”

“What? Is something the matter?”

“Of all the myriad and mischievous things you could be doing on a VPN that sooner or later is going to get caught up in the firewall—and you choose to go looking in old newspaper clippings?”

“For fuck’s sake, Tetsuya, if I wanted to be looking at porn or downloading movies, I’d be doing that at home. What kind of an exhibitionist do you think I am?”

“It’s a waste is all I’m saying.”

“And for that matter, what kind of person am I to have my hand in my pants in front of my best friend? I don’t mean to infringe on your surfing habits, but that’s where I’ll draw the line.”

Tetsuya raises his hands in the air as though I’ve cornered him at gunpoint. “Hey, you said it, not me.” He pauses and peeks behind me to grab a closer look at the screen. “What are you even working on? A research project for class?”

“Not exactly. I’m...uh...looking for someone.”

“So my best friend is a stalker. Noted. Perhaps it’s time to call it quits and visit the doctor?”

“Not like that, idiot. I mean—so, you know how a few years back, they—” I stumble on my words, “—they found my older sister, right?”

“Oh. I see.”

“And, well, I’ve been trying to figure out what happened. But I couldn’t find anything on foot. No leads. No witnesses. Digital footprint’s gone cold too. For obvious reasons, the police are also useless.”

“I understand,” Tetsuya slowly nods, his brow furrowing into a furious concentration as he pieces together my plan. “And you think we’ll get somewhere with your mark that’s coming here tonight.”

“Well, this is one of the only cafes in the city that still operates under Yomiborn turf. It’s the perfect place for us to hunt down counter-revolutionaries.”

Outside of our private PC stall, the entry bell rings, and the heavyset footsteps of a large, unwieldy group of people stumble through the foyer. All drinks and chatter fall silent in the wake of their arrival. A maniacal smirk creeps across my face.

“And who better prey to hunt than the leaders of the insurgency, the yakuza crime lords themselves?”
